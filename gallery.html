Francisca Tirona-Benitez
Little Girl of the Revolution
"I believe that every Filipina should aspire to be one who has successfully combined the art of homemaking and the pursuit of a career and still be of service of the community."
  • Birthday: June 4, 1886
  • Died: November 17, 1974
  • Province: Imus, Cavite
  • Did you know? In 1966, Francisca was elected President Emeritus of the PWU. This is a title to recognize individuals who have given up their position at an organization but continue to make significant contributions.
Francisca was a champion for women"s rights and was known for her contributions to Filipino society as an educator, humanitarian, and civic leader.
The Little Girl of the Revolution got her nickname at the age of 12 from helping wounded Katipuneros. Raised by teachers, she followed her parents" pursuit of education. After serving as an assistant dean at a girls" dormitory, she was inspired to establish a school exclusively for girls. Thus, in 1919, she founded the Philippine Women"s College and served as its first president in 1920. Now known today as Philippine Women"s University (PWU), it was the first university in Asia that was just for women.
Later on she would establish a Volunteer Social Aid Committee for war prisoners during the Japanese Occupation, establish a shelter for orphans and homeless women called Asociación de Damas Filipinas, and serve as a director of the Women"s Bureau which brought together Filipino women"s organizations and prepared them for the challenges of war.
Francisca died at the age of 88, leaving behind her array of nationally recognized awards, doctorates in philosophy and teaching, and her legacy in Filipino feminism.
c. 1960, Francisca at PWU
